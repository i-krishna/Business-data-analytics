Although this repository predominantly follows the APA (American Psychological Association) style of academic writing - commonly adopted by business schools and aligned with the conventions of my Business Analytics masters degree program - I have a deep appreciation for the IEEE citation standard, which I used extensively while writing my engineering master's thesis. IEEE's efficiency and elegance, particularly its alphanumeric approach of representing papers using the first initial letter of the authors plus the publication year (e.g., [AMMK+08]), resonates strongly with my engineering background.

It wasn't until today, during a webinar on academic writing and citation standards, that I truly appreciated how my engineering education shaped even these seemingly small choices. I trained at one of the world's leading engineering institutions, ranked 119th globally ([source](https://www.timeshighereducation.com/world-university-rankings/2025/subject-ranking/engineering?page=4#!/length/25/sort_by/rank/sort_order/asc/cols/scores)), where precision, efficiency, and clarity are core values instilled by exceptional faculty, researchers and fellow students.

This realization made me reflect on the various citation standards used across disciplines and countries. Below is a curated list of major citation styles:
